Chronic conditions
There is evidence that individuals with certain chronic health conditions may be at a higher risk of developing severe illness if they contract COVID-19. The system is able to identify patients who may be at a higher risk of developing severe illness from COVID-19 based on their chronic health conditions. These include conditions such as cardiovascular disease, diabetes, lung disease, cancer, kidney disease, obesity, and immunocompromised state...

Contact with carriers
Traveling, attending large gatherings, and coming into close contact with infected individuals are all activities that increase the risk of contracting COVID-19.

Traveling, especially by plane, train, or bus, increases the risk of exposure to the virus as these modes of transportation are often crowded and have poor ventilation.

Because the COVID-19 virus spreads easily through respiratory droplets, close contact with infected individuals increases the likelihood of exposure to these droplets and thereby risk of getting infected.
If you participated in any of these activities below in the past two weeks, please indicate:
Travel abroad
Public transport
Large gatherings
